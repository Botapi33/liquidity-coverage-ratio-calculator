What the LCR Measures

The Liquidity Coverage Ratio measures whether a bank holds enough high-quality liquid assets to withstand a 30-day stress scenario. It is one of the most important short-term liquidity metrics in banking regulation.

Why It Matters for Bond Markets

Government bonds and other highly liquid assets are central to bank liquidity management. The LCR therefore connects directly to sovereign debt markets, collateral quality and stress-period funding resilience.

Methodology

This calculator uses the standard Basel-style structure: LCR = HQLA / Net Cash Outflows over 30 days.

Net cash outflows are calculated as total stressed outflows minus recognised inflows, subject to an inflow cap.
